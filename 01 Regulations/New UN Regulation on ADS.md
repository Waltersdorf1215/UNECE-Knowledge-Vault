# New UN Regulation on ADS

## Summary

Placeholder. A new UN Regulation for Automated Driving Systems (ADS), developed by [[ADS IWG]] under [[GRVA]], intended to cover SAE Level 3+ automated driving beyond the scope of [[R157]] (ALKS). Understood to have been advanced toward adoption at [[WP29 199th Session]] (June 2026), following recommendation from [[GRVA 24th Session]] (January 2026).

*All substantive claims about scope, requirements, and adoption status are source_pending — no local source document available in this vault.*

---

## Regulatory Position

This regulation sits above [[R171]] (DCAS — assisted driving) and extends beyond [[R157]] (ALKS — limited L3 highway) in the regulatory ladder:

```
R79      → Steering / ACSF (Level 1–2 lateral)
R171     → DCAS (Level 2, combined L+L, driver retains DDT)
R157     → ALKS (Level 3, motorway, limited ODD)
This reg → ADS (Level 3+, broader ODD)       ← this note
ADS GTR  → Global harmonization under 1998 Agreement
```

---

## Relationship to ADS UN GTR

This regulation ([[New UN Regulation on ADS]]) and the [[ADS UN GTR]] are understood to be two parallel instruments developed by [[ADS IWG]]:

- This regulation: under the **1958 Agreement** → direct type-approval obligation for contracting parties (EU, Japan, Korea, UK, etc.)
- [[ADS UN GTR]]: under the **1998 Agreement** → global harmonized standard, requires national transposition
