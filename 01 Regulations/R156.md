# UN Regulation No. 156 — Software Updates and SUMS

## Summary

UN Regulation No. 156 establishes requirements for vehicle **Software Update Management Systems (SUMS)**, covering both over-the-air (OTA) and physical software updates.

**Fact (R171, §10.3):** "The vehicle manufacturer shall demonstrate compliance with UN Regulation No. 156 (Software Update and Software Update Management System) by fulfilling the requirements and respecting the transitional provisions of the original version of UN Regulation No. 156 or later series of amendments." This is a confirmed explicit cross-reference: [[R171]] **requires** R156 compliance.

*Interpretation:* R155 and R156 are widely understood as enabling conditions for ADS deployment, and the ADS UN GTR requires equivalent cybersecurity (§4.3.2) and software update management (§4.3.3) processes. Whether R155 and R156 type approvals are formal prerequisites for R157 type approval requires reading the R157 text [VERIFY].

---

## Scope

Requires OEMs to establish and maintain a certified SUMS:
- Traceability of software versions across the fleet lifecycle
- Controlled update processes (OTA and physical)
- Verification that updates do not invalidate type-approval compliance
- Cybersecurity impact assessment for every update (linking back to [[R155]])

The [[Software Update]] concept note provides definitional context.

---

## Role in the ADAS/ADS Regulatory Stack

R156 is a **procedural enabler**: it governs how software changes are managed, ensuring that a type-approved ADS system (under [[R157]] or the [[New UN Regulation on ADS]]) remains compliant after field updates.

```
R155 (CSMS: cybersecurity risk management)
R156 (SUMS: software update governance)     ← this note
     ↓ both are prerequisites for:
R157 / New UN Regulation on ADS
```

*Interpretation:* For ADS vehicles that rely on continuous software updates (e.g., OTA to ML models), R156 is particularly critical. Every update to the ADS software must be traceable and assessed for type-approval impact.

---

## Relationship to ADS 2026 Framework

The [[New UN Regulation on ADS]] and [[ADS UN GTR]] are expected to require R156 compliance. ADS systems that use OTA updates to modify driving behavior must maintain a certified SUMS.

*Interpretation:* This is especially relevant for OEMs like [[Tesla]], [[NIO]], and [[Xpeng]] that rely heavily on OTA update models.
