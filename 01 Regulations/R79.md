# UN Regulation No. 79 — Steering Equipment

## Summary

UN Regulation No. 79 governs vehicle steering equipment. It has been progressively amended to introduce **Automatically Commanded Steering Functions (ACSF)** — covering automated lateral control assistance from simple lane keeping through to automated lane changes. R79 is the foundational regulation for lateral vehicle control, sitting at the base of the UNECE automated driving regulatory ladder.

---

## Regulatory Position

```
R79    → Lateral assistance (ACSF categories A–E)   ← this note
R171   → Combined lateral + longitudinal (DCAS, L2)
R157   → Automated lane keeping (ALKS, L3)
```

---

## ACSF Categories (source_pending — verify against official text)

ACSF categories introduced through R79 amendments define escalating levels of automated lateral assistance:

| Category | Description | Approx. SAE Equivalent |
|---|---|---|
| A | Corrective steering (brief corrections) | L1 |
| B | Lane keep assist (continuous lateral) | L1 |
| B1 | Emergency steering function | L1 |
| C | Lane change function (driver-initiated) | L2 |
| D | Lane change function (system-initiated, driver confirmation) | L2 |
| E | Automated lane change (system-initiated, no active confirmation) | L2+ |

*Interpretation:* ACSF Category E is the highest steering automation in R79 and forms the direct predecessor to the Level 3 ALKS scope of [[R157]].

---

## Relevance to ADS Framework

R79 underpins the steering control requirements for all higher-level automated driving regulations:
- [[R157]] ALKS presupposes R79 ACSF compliance for lateral control
- The [[New UN Regulation on ADS]] will need to address how R79's ACSF framework applies — or whether it is superseded — for full ADS lateral control

*Interpretation:* A gap analysis is needed: whether R79's ACSF framework needs amendment to accommodate ADS-commanded steering where no human fallback for lateral control is expected during the [[ODD]].

## Confirmed Cross-References from Other Regulations

**Fact (R171, §5.1.5):** R171 requires vehicles equipped with DCAS to comply with R79 (Corrective Steering Function), along with R130, R131, and R152.

**Fact (R171, §5.3.7.3.1):** R171 requires its Risk Mitigation Function (RMF) to comply with "the 04 or later series of amendments to UN Regulation No. 79."

These establish confirmed Class 1 (Official) relationships:
- [[R171]] → `requires` → R79 (Corrective Steering Function)
- [[R171]] → `requires` → R79 (04-series RMF provisions)
