# UN Regulation No. 155 — Cybersecurity and CSMS

## Summary

UN Regulation No. 155 establishes requirements for vehicle **Cybersecurity Management Systems (CSMS)**. It is one of three foundational regulations — together with [[R156]] (software updates) and [[R157]] (ALKS) — that form the UNECE automated driving enablement framework. R155 compliance is a precondition for [[R157]] type approval, and is expected to be a precondition for the [[New UN Regulation on ADS]].

---

## Scope

Requires OEMs to establish, maintain, and certify a **CSMS** covering the full vehicle lifecycle:
- Cybersecurity risk assessment and management
- Threat detection and incident response
- Post-production monitoring
- Supplier cybersecurity requirements

The [[Cybersecurity]] concept note provides definitional context.

---

## Role in the ADAS/ADS Regulatory Stack

R155 is a **foundational enabler**, not a function-specific regulation. It applies across all vehicles with automated or connected functions:

```
R155 (CSMS) ─────────────────────────────────┐
R156 (SUMS) ─────────────────────────────────┤→ prerequisites for ADS type approval
                                              │
R157 / New UN Regulation on ADS ─────────────┘
```

*Interpretation:* No ADS type approval is possible without a valid R155 CSMS certificate. This makes R155 a gating requirement for the entire ADS regulatory pathway.

---

## Relationship to ADS 2026 Framework

The [[New UN Regulation on ADS]] and [[ADS UN GTR]] are expected to cross-reference R155 as a precondition. When type approval for ADS is sought under the new regulation, an existing approved CSMS under R155 will be required.
