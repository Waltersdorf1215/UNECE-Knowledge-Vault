# UN Regulation No. 171 — Driver Control Assistance Systems (DCAS)

## Summary

**Fact (E/ECE/TRANS/505/Rev.3/Add.170, cover page):** UN Regulation No. 171 establishes uniform provisions concerning the approval of vehicles with regard to **Driver Control Assistance Systems (DCAS)**. It entered into force as an annex to the 1958 Agreement on **22 September 2024**. The authentic legal text is **ECE/TRANS/WP.29/2024/37**.

## Official Definitions (§2)

**Fact (§2.1):** "**Driver Control Assistance System (DCAS)** means the hardware and software collectively capable of assisting a driver in controlling the longitudinal and lateral motion of the vehicle on a sustained basis."

**Fact (§2.4):** "**Dynamic Control** means the real-time performance of operational and tactical functions required to move the vehicle. … For the purpose of this UN Regulation, only a driver is in charge and responsible for vehicle dynamic control whereas DCAS provides assistance to carry out operational and tactical functions without limiting the driver's ability to intervene at any given time."

**Fact (§2.5):** "**System Boundaries** are those verifiable or measurable limits or conditions established by a manufacturer up to or within which DCAS or a feature of DCAS is designed to provide assistance."

**Fact (§2.6):** "**Driver disengagement** means the system's determination of the driver's current inability to safely execute perception, planning, or decision-making and to intervene in the operation of DCAS."

**Fact (§2.32):** "**Automated Driving System (ADS)** means the vehicle hardware and software that are collectively capable of performing the entire Dynamic Driving Task (DDT) on a sustained basis." *(same definition as ADS UN GTR §2.1)*

---

## Scope

**Fact (§1.1):** Applies to the type approval of vehicles of **Categories M and N** with regard to their DCAS.

**Fact (§1.2):** Does NOT apply to ACSF or RMF approved under [[R79]], unless the manufacturer declares such systems to be part of DCAS.

---

## SAE Level Classification

**Fact (Introduction §4):** "According to the standard SAE J3016…DCAS are treated as 'SAE level 2 according to SAE J3016' (partial automation), systems that are only capable of performing parts of the vehicle dynamic control, and thus require a driver to perform the remainder of dynamic control, as well as to supervise the system operation and vehicle environment."

---

## ADS Distinction — Officially Stated

**Fact (Introduction §5):** "While both DCAS and Automated Driving Systems (ADS) of higher automation levels 3 to 5 according to SAE J3016 provide lateral and longitudinal control on a sustained basis, only ADS may permit the driver to disengage from the driving task, as only ADS, by definition, is capable of managing all driving situations reasonably expected within their Operational Design Domain (ODD) without further input from the driver. Instead, DCAS only assist the driver but never replace the driver. As a consequence, there is no transfer in the driver's responsibility for control of the vehicle."

---

## Regulatory Position

*Interpretation:* R171 covers SAE Level 2 (DCAS) and fills a gap above R79's ACSF provisions. The introduction (§3) explicitly states the regulation "aims to allow the approval of a variety of driver control assistance features, filling an existing regulatory gap" beyond the "03 series of amendments to UN Regulation No. 79". No official UNECE document positions R171 relative to R157 (ALKS) as a "companion" or "predecessor" — see `11 Review/Pending/R157-R171-evidence-review.md`.

## DCAS Features (§6 and §9.1.1)

**Fact (§9.1.1 table):** R171 defines five DCAS feature types:
1. Positioning in the lane of travel
2. Driver-initiated lane change
3. Driver-confirmed lane change
4. System-initiated lane change
5. Other manoeuvres (turns, roundabouts, obstruction avoidance, parking)

**Fact (§9.1.2):** Features may operate in **Highway** domain, **Non-Highway** domain, or both. Declared per feature type.

## Key Requirements

### Prerequisite Systems (§5.1.5) — EXPLICIT CROSS-REFERENCES

**Fact (§5.1.5):** "The DCAS-equipped vehicle shall at least be equipped with an **Advanced Emergency Braking System**. In addition, it shall be equipped with either a **Lane Departure Prevention System** or **Lane Departure Warning System**. These systems shall comply with the technical requirements and transitional provisions of **UN Regulations Nos. 131, 152, 79** (Corrective Steering Function) **and 130**, as appropriate for the DCAS-equipped vehicle category."

This establishes confirmed Class 1 (Official) relationships:
- R171 → **requires** → [[R131]] (AEBS — heavy vehicles)
- R171 → **requires** → [[R152]] (AEBS — light vehicles)
- R171 → **requires** → [[R79]] (Corrective Steering Function)
- R171 → **requires** → [[R130]] (LDWS / Lane Departure Prevention)

### Software Updates (§10.3) — EXPLICIT CROSS-REFERENCE

**Fact (§10.3):** "The vehicle manufacturer shall demonstrate compliance with **UN Regulation No. 156** (Software Update and Software Update Management System) by fulfilling the requirements and respecting the transitional provisions of the original version of UN Regulation No. 156 or later series of amendments."

This establishes a confirmed Class 1 (Official) relationship:
- R171 → **requires** → [[R156]] (Software Updates)

### Risk Mitigation Function (§5.3.7.3)

**Fact (§5.3.7.3.1):** "The system shall comply with the technical requirements and transitional provisions of the **04 or later series of amendments to UN Regulation No. 79** with respect to the **Risk Mitigation Function (RMF)**. In the event that the driver has been determined to be unavailable following a driver disengagement warning escalation sequence…the system shall appropriately activate the Risk Mitigation Function in order to come to a safe stop."

See [[Risk Mitigation Function]].

### Driver State Monitoring (§5.5.4.2)

**Fact (§5.5.4.2.1):** DCAS must monitor both **motoric disengagement** (hands on steering control) and **visual disengagement** (eye gaze / head posture).

**Fact (§5.5.4.2.6) — Warning escalation sequence:**

| Step | Trigger | Type | Timing |
|---|---|---|---|
| HOR | Motorically disengaged >5s (or >5s if visually engaged) | Hands On Request (visual) | Latest 5s after disengagement detected |
| Escalated HOR | No response to HOR | HOR + acoustic/haptic | Latest 10s after initial HOR |
| EOR | Visually disengaged >5s | Eyes On Request (visual + other modality) | Latest 5s after visual disengagement |
| Escalated EOR | No response to EOR | Increased intensity (must include acoustic/haptic) | Latest 3s after initial EOR |
| DCA | No response to escalated EOR | Direct Control Alert (visual + other modality) | Latest 5s after escalated EOR |
| Driver Unavailability Response | No response to DCA | Activate RMF → safe stop | Latest 10s after first escalated request/alert |

See [[Driver Monitoring]] and [[Risk Mitigation Function]].

### Speed Range

**Fact (§5.3.7.4.4):** "The maximum speed up to which the system and any of its features provides assistance shall not exceed the maximum speed limit in the country where the vehicle is currently operating."

No explicit minimum or maximum speed is fixed by the regulation for DCAS overall — these are declared per feature by the manufacturer (§9.1.1). However, warning escalation applies "at speeds above 10 km/h" (§5.5.4.2.6.1.1).

### General Safety (§5.1.5 summary)

**Fact (§5.1.1):** "The system shall be designed to ensure the driver remains engaged with the driving task."

**Fact (§5.1.4):** "The system shall provide the driver a means to safely override or deactivate the system at any time."

## System Validation — Multi-Pillar (§8)

**Fact (§8.2):** Validation must include:
- (a) Audit of safety aspects per Annex 3
- (b) Physical tests (test track and public roads) per Annex 4
- (c) In-service monitoring per §7

**Fact (§8.2.1):** Virtual testing is permitted; requires a credibility assessment per Annex 5.

---

## In-Service Monitoring (§7)

**Fact (§7.2.1.1):** Manufacturer must notify the Type Approval Authority of any **Safety-Critical Occurrence** as soon as practical.

**Fact (§7.2.3.1):** Periodic reporting: at least **once a year** to the Type Approval Authority. Required data includes: safety-critical occurrences, fleet distance driven, driver unavailability responses, system-initiated deactivations (failures vs. boundary exceedances).

---

## GDPR / Data Protection Implications

R171 is a UNECE vehicle type-approval regulation. It is not itself a GDPR instrument. Its GDPR relevance arises where DCAS driver monitoring, in-service monitoring, safety-critical occurrence notification, or reporting data are processed in a way that relates to an identified or identifiable natural person in the EU/EEA.

**Fact (R171, §5.5.4.2.1.1):** DCAS monitors whether the driver is motorically disengaged (hands removed from steering control) and visually disengaged (eye gaze or head posture away from driving-task-relevant areas).

**Fact (R171, §7.2.3.1):** Periodic reporting to the Type Approval Authority includes safety-critical occurrences, fleet distance driven, driver unavailability responses, and system-initiated deactivations.

**Fact (GDPR, Article 4(1), official EU text):** Personal data includes information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person, including identifiers such as identification number, location data, online identifier, or factors specific to that person's physical, physiological, mental, economic, cultural, or social identity.

**Fact (GDPR, Article 4(2), official EU text):** Processing includes collection, recording, storage, use, disclosure by transmission, and erasure.

**Fact (GDPR, Article 5(1), official EU text):** Personal data must be processed lawfully, fairly, transparently, for specified purposes, limited to what is necessary, stored no longer than necessary, and secured against unauthorised or unlawful processing.

**Fact (GDPR, Article 6(1), official EU text):** Processing is lawful only if at least one legal basis applies, including consent, contract necessity, legal obligation, public interest/official authority, vital interests, or legitimate interests where applicable.

**Fact (GDPR, Article 25, official EU text):** Controllers must implement data protection by design and by default, including measures such as pseudonymisation and ensuring that only personal data necessary for each specific purpose are processed by default.

**Fact (GDPR, Article 35, official EU text):** A data protection impact assessment is required where processing using new technologies is likely to result in high risk to natural persons, and must assess necessity, proportionality, risks, and mitigation measures.

*Interpretation:* If R171 driver-monitoring signals, driver unavailability responses, safety-critical occurrences, or in-service monitoring records can be linked to a driver, vehicle user, VIN-associated person, account, location trace, or other identifier, they should be treated as potentially personal data under GDPR. Implementers should define a lawful basis, minimize retained data, separate safety/type-approval reporting from secondary analytics, set retention periods, secure access, and document controller/processor responsibilities.

*Interpretation:* R171's annual and event-based reporting duties do not automatically justify collecting all raw in-cabin monitoring data. A GDPR-aligned implementation should prefer aggregated, event-limited, pseudonymised, or anonymised reporting where those forms are sufficient for R171 safety monitoring and type-approval purposes.

*Interpretation:* A DPIA is likely to be relevant for EU DCAS deployments where driver monitoring uses cameras, gaze/head-pose inference, persistent event logs, telematics upload, or systematic behaviour monitoring at scale. This is especially important if data are transmitted off-board to manufacturers, technical services, type approval authorities, cloud providers, or analytics vendors.
