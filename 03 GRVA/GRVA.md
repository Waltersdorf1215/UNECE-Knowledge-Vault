# GRVA — Working Party on Automated/Autonomous and Connected Vehicles

## Summary

GRVA (Working Party on Automated/Autonomous and Connected Vehicles) is the UNECE subsidiary body under [[WP29]] responsible for developing and maintaining the full regulatory stack for automated and connected vehicles. It is the principal forum where regulations on automated driving are drafted, debated, and recommended for [[WP29]] adoption.

---

## Governance

```
WP.29 (World Forum for Harmonization of Vehicle Regulations)
  └── GRVA (Working Party on Automated/Autonomous and Connected Vehicles)
        ├── ADS IWG (Automated Driving Systems IWG)
        │     ├── FRAV (Functional Requirements for Automated Vehicles)
        │     ├── VMAD (Validation Method for Automated Driving)
        │     └── DSSAD (Data Storage System for Automated Driving)
        ├── DCAS / ADAS TF (Driver Control Assistance / ADAS Task Force)
        └── EDR-DSSAD (Event Data Recorder)
```

GRVA reports to [[WP29]] at each of WP.29's sessions. Formal regulations recommended by GRVA are submitted to WP.29 for adoption.

## ADS Development Track (2026 Regulatory Cycle)

The [[ADS IWG]] — operating under GRVA — has been developing both a new UN Regulation for ADS and the [[ADS UN GTR]]. The [[GRVA 24th Session]] (January 2026) is understood to have been a key milestone in recommending these instruments to [[WP29]].

Key sub-groups and their roles:
- [[ADS IWG]] — overall ADS regulation architecture and drafting
- [[FRAV]] — functional requirements (mandate largely completed; outputs feed into ADS regulation)
- [[VMAD]] — validation methodology (ongoing; critical for AI-based ADS validation)
- [[DSSAD]] / [[EDR-DSSAD]] — data recording requirements

---

## ADAS Track

Parallel to the ADS work, GRVA manages the ADAS regulatory track:
- [[DCAS]] working group developed [[R171]]
- [[ADAS TF]] handles ongoing R171 amendments (Supplement 2 proposal exists locally: `raw/ADAS-37-02`)
