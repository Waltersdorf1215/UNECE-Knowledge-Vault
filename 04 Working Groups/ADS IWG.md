# ADS IWG — Informal Working Group on Automated Driving Systems

## Summary

**Fact:** The IWG on ADS is the Informal Working Group established under [[GRVA]] to develop the regulatory framework for Automated Driving Systems (ADS) at SAE Level 3 and above. It developed two parallel instruments: the [[ADS UN GTR]] (1998 Agreement) and the [[New UN Regulation on ADS]] (1958 Agreement), recommended to [[WP29 199th Session]] at [[GRVA 24th Session]].

---

## Establishment

**Fact (from ECE/TRANS/WP.29/2026/139, §13–15):**
- **191st WP.29 session (November 2023):** WP.29 adopted the proposal for regulatory approach for ADS (WP.29-191-30/Rev.1), creating the IWG on ADS and GRVA workshops on ADS.
- **192nd WP.29 session (March 2024):** WP.29 formally agreed on IWG on ADS leadership; AC.3 authorized the new UN GTR.

- **SAE International**

**Ambassadors** between IWG on ADS and GRVA Workshops: **Australia** and **Netherlands**

---

## Work Structure

**Fact (§16):** The IWG reviewed a draft structural framework with sections:
- "General requirements"
- "Performance requirements / Test specifications"
- "Assessment / Test procedures"

**Officers of Principal Interest (OPI)** appointed per section as coordinators.

**Two phases (§17–19):**
1. Transposition of ADS guidelines into common regulatory provisions (requirements, then assessment methods)
2. Separation of common provisions into GTR and UN Regulation texts; integration of GRVA ADS workshop outcomes

---

## Technical Basis

**Fact:** The IWG's work is derived from the **FRAV/VMAD Integrated Document** (ECE/TRANS/WP.29/2024/39, approved June 2024), which consolidated [[FRAV]] functional requirements and [[VMAD]] validation methodology.
