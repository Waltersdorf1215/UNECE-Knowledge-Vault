# VMAD — Validation Method for Automated Driving

## Summary

**Fact:** VMAD (Validation Method for Automated Driving) is the informal working group under [[GRVA]] that developed the validation methodology framework for ADS. Its key outputs — culminating in the FRAV/VMAD Integrated Document (ECE/TRANS/WP.29/2024/39) — form the primary technical basis for the [[ADS UN GTR]].

---

## Mandate

**Fact (from ECE/TRANS/WP.29/2026/139, §10):**
> "The VMAD Terms of Reference (ToR) mandated the group to develop assessment methods, including scenarios, to validate the safety of automated systems based on a multi-pillar approach including audit, simulation/virtual testing, test track, and real-world testing."

---

## Key Deliverables

**Fact (§10):** VMAD contributed to the **NATM** (New Assessment/Test Method for Automated Driving) master document:
- **NATM first version:** adopted at 184th WP.29 session (June 2021) as ECE/TRANS/WP.29/1159
- **NATM second version:** submitted to GRVA 12th session (January 2022) as ECE/TRANS/WP29/GRVA/2022/2

**Fact (§11–12):** VMAD and FRAV were instructed to develop the NATM guidelines (ECE/TRANS/WP.29/2022/57). The FRAV/VMAD Integrated Document was approved at the June 2024 WP.29 session (ECE/TRANS/WP.29/2024/39).

---

## Multi-Pillar Validation Approach

**Fact (§34–39):** VMAD's framework established four validation pillars, all reflected in the GTR:

| Pillar | Description | GTR Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Virtual testing | Simulation toolchains | §5.2.1, §6.2.1 |
| Track testing | Closed-access proving ground | §5.2.2, §6.2.2 |
| Real-world testing | Public road testing | §5.2.3, §6.2.3 |
| Audit | SMS and safety case audit | §6.1, §6.3 |

---

## ODD-Based Behavioural Competencies (Annex 5)

**Fact:** VMAD's approach includes ODD-based behavioural competency identification and scenario generation — formalised in Annex 5 of the GTR. Key steps:
1. ODD analysis
2. Driving interactions analysis
3. OEDR analysis → behavioural competency identification
4. Scenario identification (functional → abstract → logical → concrete)
5. Competency mapping (nominal / critical / failure situations)

Scenario generation uses **knowledge-based**, **data-based**, and **goal-based** methods.

---

## Relationship to FRAV

[[FRAV]] defines *what* ADS must do (functional requirements). VMAD defines *how to prove it* (validation methodology). Both contributed to ECE/TRANS/WP.29/2024/39.
