# FRAV — Functional Requirements for Automated Vehicles

## Summary

**Fact:** FRAV (Functional Requirements for Automated Vehicles) is the informal working group under [[GRVA]] that developed functional (performance) requirements for ADS. Its outputs — together with [[VMAD]]'s validation methodology — form the FRAV/VMAD Integrated Document (ECE/TRANS/WP.29/2024/39), the primary technical basis for the [[ADS UN GTR]].

---

## Mandate

**Fact (from ECE/TRANS/WP.29/2026/139, §10):**
> "FRAV developed functional (performance) requirements for automated vehicles."

**Fact (§9):** The Framework Document (ECE/TRANS/WP.29/2019/34/Rev.2) allocated FRAV the work of developing a "new assessment/test method for automated driving" (NATM), collaborating with VMAD.

---

## Key Deliverables

**Fact:**
- Contributed to **NATM** (New Assessment/Test Method for Automated Driving)
- **NATM first version:** adopted at 184th WP.29 (June 2021), ECE/TRANS/WP.29/1159
- **FRAV/VMAD Integrated Document:** approved at June 2024 WP.29 session, **ECE/TRANS/WP.29/2024/39** — this is the foundational technical document for the GTR

**Fact (§12):** "WP.29 approved the integrated FRAV/VMAD guidelines during the June 2024 session."

**Fact (§17):** "This text is derived from the specific provisions and annexes received from the June 2024 Functional Requirements for Automated Vehicles (FRAV) - Validation Method for Automated Driving (VMAD) Informal Working Group Integrated Document."

---

## Content in GTR

FRAV's functional requirements are reflected throughout the [[ADS UN GTR]], particularly:
- **Section 4 (ADS requirements):** DDT performance in nominal, critical, and failure situations
- **Section 4.1.5 (ODD boundaries):** ODD recognition and fallback
- **Annex 5 (ODD-based Behavioural Competencies):** Directly derived from FRAV/VMAD work
