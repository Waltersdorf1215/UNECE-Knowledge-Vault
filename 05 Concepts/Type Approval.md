# Type Approval

## Summary

Type Approval (also: homologation) is the regulatory process by which a vehicle model or component is certified to meet applicable technical standards before being placed on the market. In the UNECE context, type approval under the **1958 Agreement** grants an E-mark that is mutually recognized across contracting parties.

---

## Relevance to ADS Regulation

Type approval is the primary mechanism through which the UNECE ADS regulatory framework will be enforced:
- [[New UN Regulation on ADS]] — creates ADS type-approval requirements under the 1958 Agreement
- [[R155]] — CSMS must be approved before ADS type approval is granted
- [[R156]] — SUMS must be approved before ADS type approval is granted
- [[R157]] — ALKS type approval under the 1958 Agreement (current L3 framework)

---

## 1958 vs. 1998 Agreement

| Feature | 1958 Agreement | 1998 Agreement |
|---|---|---|
| Output | UN Regulation → type approval obligation | UN GTR → performance standard (must be transposed) |
| ADS instrument | [[New UN Regulation on ADS]] | [[ADS UN GTR]] |
| Key parties | EU, Japan, Korea, UK, Russia, etc. | US (NHTSA), EU, China, Japan, India, etc. |
| US included? | No | Yes |
| Enforcement | Direct — type approval denied if non-compliant | Indirect — requires national transposition |

## ADS Type Approval Pathway (source_pending)

For a vehicle to receive ADS type approval under the [[New UN Regulation on ADS]]:
1. Demonstrate [[R155]] CSMS approval
2. Demonstrate [[R156]] SUMS approval
3. Demonstrate [[VMAD]]-compliant validation evidence
4. Demonstrate [[DSSAD]] / [[Event Data Recorder]] compliance
5. Demonstrate ADS functional safety and [[SOTIF]] compliance within [[ODD]]
