# ISMR — In-Service Monitoring and Reporting

## Definition

**Fact (ECE/TRANS/WP.29/2026/139, §5.1.8.1):**
ISMR is the manufacturer's process to monitor ADS performance post-deployment, carried out as part of the [[Safety Management System]].

---

## Purpose

**Fact (§5.1.8.1):** Demonstrate capability to:
- Monitor ADS operations
- Confirm compliance with the safety case
- Identify safety risks requiring SMS action
- Manage safety-relevant gaps in operation
- Develop new scenarios or revise existing ones
- Report occurrences to the relevant authority

---

## Three Reporting Types (§5.4)

**Fact:**

| Type | Trigger | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| Initial notification | Critical occurrence | Without unreasonable delay |
| Short-term report | Critical + significant occurrences (Annex 1) | Within 30 days |
| Periodic report | All occurrences (Annex 1) | At least annually |

---

## Significant Occurrences Requiring Short-term Reporting (Annex 1)

**Fact (Annex 1):**
- ADS operation outside ODD
- Failure to achieve MRC when necessary
- Failure to meet GTR Section 4 requirements
- Performance issues constituting unreasonable risk

---

## DSSAD as ISMR Foundation

**Fact (§4.3.1 and Annex 6):** The [[DSSAD]] installed on every ADS vehicle provides the data foundation for ISMR, recording activation, deactivation, ODD exits, MRC fallbacks, collisions, and emergency manoeuvres.
